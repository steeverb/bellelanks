Citi acquired mortgage.com to expand its share of the U.S. mortgage market and turn the URL into a credible front door for first-time buyers. My role spanned both halves of the work: editing the educational content the audience would land on, then overseeing a redesign aimed at converting those readers into mortgage applicants.

Context

Mortgage.com was being rebuilt as Citi's content hub for prospective borrowers — a place to demystify the process of buying a home and meet readers where their search queries had taken them. The challenge: educational content can be helpful and still convert badly, and conversion-optimized copy can convert and still feel like marketing. The brief was to make both work at once.

The editorial layer

The articles needed to read as trustworthy independent guidance, not pre-loan sales copy. That meant tightening voice, surfacing the questions real first-time buyers ask, and making the prose plain enough to be useful without being condescending.

  • Established a voice and standards baseline for educational pieces
  • Edited [X] core articles to clarify reading level, structure, and stakes
  • Built an editorial review loop with subject-matter reviewers

The redesign

The redesign reorganized the site around the reader's actual journey — from "I'm thinking about buying" through "I'm ready to apply" — instead of around Citi's product taxonomy. Calls-to-action moved closer to the moments of intent. Friction came out of the apply flow.
